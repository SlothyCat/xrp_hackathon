The UN Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (OCHA) coordinates life-saving aid across global emergencies, leveraging anticipatory action, rapid funding pools, and local partnerships to reach the most vulnerable.

Anticipatory Action

OCHA’s Anticipatory Action framework uses reliable forecasts and pre-arranged finance to trigger early interventions—such as pre-positioning food, cash transfers, and shelter supplies—before disasters strike, reaching millions in countries like Bangladesh, Chad, and the Philippines :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

Cash-for-Work Initiative for Rohingya Refugees

In Cox’s Bazar, nearly 5,000 Rohingya refugees have been engaged in cash-for-work activities— from clearing drainage channels to building bamboo bridges—earning daily wages that restore dignity and provide crucial household income :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}.

Flexible Funds 2025 – Gaza Response

The UN’s Flexible Funds mechanism, including the Central Emergency Response Fund (CERF), swiftly allocates unearmarked financing to partners in Gaza, enabling the entry and distribution of an average of 50 truckloads of aid per day amid shifting operational constraints :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}.
